# Contract Review Workflow

## Overall Review

1. Establish basics: contract type, parties, review perspective, governing law, dispute venue, term, amount, and transaction purpose.
2. Read key clauses: subject matter, price/payment, delivery, acceptance, obligations, warranties, breach liability, termination, confidentiality, IP, data compliance, dispute resolution, signing/effectiveness.
3. Search legal/company/template evidence for the contract type and high-risk terms.
4. Produce a risk table with clause/location, issue, consequence, evidence, and fix.
5. Add missing clauses and materials to request.

## Clause-by-Clause Review

For each clause in scope, assign one result:

- 通过: no material issue based on available evidence.
- 需修改: issue exists but can be fixed through wording.
- 高风险: likely material legal/commercial exposure or enforceability issue.
- 无法判断: missing contract context, attachment, authorization, or legal/company evidence.

Check each clause for:

- Legality and mandatory-rule conflict.
- Balance of rights and obligations.
- Operability: deadlines, acceptance criteria, notice paths, evidence.
- Conflict with other clauses.
- Ambiguity, undefined terms, missing cross-references.
- Unreasonable breach cost, unilateral discretion, or hidden liability.

## Revision Drafting

Each revision suggestion must include:

- `original_text`: exact excerpt or "原文未提供".
- `suggested_text`: formal contract language ready to paste.
- `reason`: why the change reduces risk.
- `negotiation_impact`: whether it is likely neutral, mild, or material in negotiation.
- `needs_business_confirmation`: mark variables such as amount, cap, term, percentage, service level, jurisdiction, or notice period.

Do not silently change commercial substance. If a change shifts deal economics or risk allocation, flag it.

## Incremental Review

Compare old/new versions by clause topic:

- New clause.
- Deleted clause.
- Modified clause.
- Non-material formatting/numbering change.

Review only material changes. Do not repeat old risks unless the new version expands, hides, weakens, or transfers them.

Prioritize changes involving:

- Payment, price, deposit, refund, invoice.
- Delivery, acceptance, delay, title/risk transfer.
- Liability cap, indemnity, liquidated damages.
- Termination, suspension, unilateral change.
- Confidentiality, IP ownership/license.
- Personal information/data/security compliance.
- Governing law, court/arbitration, jurisdiction.
- Signing, effective date, condition precedent.
