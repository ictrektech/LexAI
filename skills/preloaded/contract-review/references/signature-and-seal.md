# Party, Authorization, Signature, Seal, and Effectiveness

## Party Consistency

Check:

- Full legal name.
- Unified social credit code.
- Address, contact, bank account, invoice information.
- Whether names match across title, preamble, signature block, attachments, PO, invoice, authorization, and seal.

If public registry or internal master data is unavailable, do not infer business status. Mark it as a verification item.

## Authorization

Check whether the signer is:

- Legal representative.
- Branch/company responsible person.
- Authorized representative with power of attorney.

Power of attorney should cover:

- Contract type and transaction.
- Amount or authority limit.
- Signing act.
- Validity period.
- Principal name and seal/signature.

## Signature and Seal

Review:

- Company seal, contract seal, legal representative seal, personal signature, electronic signature.
- Whether seal type matches the transaction and company rules.
- Whether signature page is attached to the same contract text.
- Whether attachments, schedules, quotation, SOW, technical specs, and acceptance forms need seal/signature.
- Whether date is present and consistent.
- Whether scanned copy has alteration, overlay, page replacement, or incomplete image concerns.

Do not state that a seal or signature is genuine unless evidence proves it.

## Effectiveness

Do not equate "signed/sealed" with "effective". Check:

- Effective upon signature/seal.
- Effective upon approval, payment, delivery, filing, notarization, board/shareholder approval, or platform confirmation.
- Whether all conditions precedent are satisfied.
- Whether counterpart signing, electronic signing process, or signing order matters.

## Output

For party/signature review, produce:

- Party consistency conclusion.
- Authorization and signing risk.
- Seal/signature risk.
- Effectiveness condition risk.
- Missing materials and verification steps.
