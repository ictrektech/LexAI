# Risk Levels and Prioritization

## High Risk

Use high risk when the clause or missing material may cause major legal, financial, enforceability, or execution exposure.

Common triggers:

- Violates mandatory legal rules or likely invalid/unenforceable.
- Missing or inconsistent party identity, authorization, signature, seal, or effectiveness condition.
- Payment, delivery, acceptance, termination, or liability clause is one-sided and materially adverse to the review perspective.
- Liability is uncapped, disproportionate, duplicated, or triggered too easily.
- Dispute venue or governing law is materially unfavorable or unclear.
- Key attachment is absent: SOW, specifications, price list, PO, acceptance criteria, data processing terms, authorization.
- Personal information, data export, confidentiality, or IP terms are missing for a data/IP-heavy transaction.

## Medium Risk

Use medium risk when the issue is meaningful but usually negotiable or fixable by wording.

Common triggers:

- Ambiguous deadline, acceptance procedure, notice method, or deliverable standard.
- Undefined terms or inconsistent cross-references.
- Missing evidence-retention mechanism.
- Remedies exist but are incomplete or hard to operate.
- Termination and transition obligations are under-specified.
- Warranty, support, confidentiality, or IP scope is too broad/narrow but can be refined.

## Low Risk

Use low risk for minor drafting or housekeeping issues that are unlikely to block signing.

Common triggers:

- Typo, numbering inconsistency, minor formatting issue.
- Non-material wording ambiguity.
- Missing optional operational detail.
- Duplicative but non-conflicting statement.

## Prioritization

When producing a negotiation order:

1. High-risk validity, authority, payment, delivery, acceptance, liability, termination, dispute resolution.
2. Medium-risk operational clauses and evidence mechanisms.
3. Low-risk drafting cleanup.

Always explain risk from the chosen review perspective. A clause favorable to Party A may be unfavorable to Party B.
